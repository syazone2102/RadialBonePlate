Prediction Methodology

This calculator operates as a Clinical Decision Support System (CDSS). The prediction engine relies on scaling empirical Finite Element Analysis (FEA) data to patient-specific dynamic loads.

1. The FEA Baseline

The system anchors on an extracted FEA reference datum indicating a maximum nodal displacement of 8.255 mm under a standardized 500 N physiological load, utilizing a standard 2.5 mm Ti-6Al-4V construct.

2. Force Scaling (Linear)

Assuming the Titanium alloy and cortical bone remain within their linear elastic limits, in vivo force is calculated as a product of patient mass, gravity, and the UCLA activity multiplier (dynamic shock load).

3. Stiffness Scaling (Cubic)

To accurately simulate customized plate thickness, the engine applies the structural engineering principle of the Area Moment of Inertia ($I = \frac{bh^3}{12}$). Bending resistance increases cubically ($h^3$) with plate thickness, exponentially reducing predicted micromotion.
